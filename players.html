LIMERICK • 1930–1947

Mick Mackey

The driving force behind Limerick's golden age, Mackey's athleticism and fearless style transformed what many believed a hurler could achieve.

Defining Moment

Led Limerick to three All-Ireland titles during one of the greatest periods in the county's history.

Cork • 1939-1963

Christy Ring

Widely regarded as the greatest hurler of all time, Ring combined skill, power and elegance to inspire generations.

Defining Moment

Scored six goals in a single Munster Final and won eight All-Ireland titles with Cork.

Wexford • 1940s-1950s

Nicky Rackard

Wexford's greatest hurler, Rackard's scoring exploits and leadership helped elevate the game to new heights.

Defining Moment

Led Wexford to the 1955 All-Ireland title and became one of the game's first national heroes.

Tipperary • 1957-1973

Jimmy Doyle

A scoring machine whose accuracy & consistency made Doyle the benchmark for generations of forwards.

Defining Moment

Retired as the highest scorer in championship history, a record that stood for decades.

Offaly • 1989-2006

Brian Whelahan

Whelahan's versatility, leadership and determination made him one of Offaly's greatest-ever players.

Defining Moment

Played full back & full forward in dramatic 1998 All-Ireland Final victory where he was named MOTM.

Kilkenny • 1999-2015

Henry Shefflin

Known as "King Henry", Shefflin's leadership, composure and relentless scoring helped define one of the greatest dynasties in hurling history.

Defining Moment

Won a record ten All-Ireland medals and became the face of Kilkenny's golden era.

Galway • 2008-2021

Joe Canning

A modern icon whose skill, vision and spectacular scoring ability inspired a new generation of hurling supporters both in Galway and beyond.

Defining Moment

Inspired Galway to its first All-Ireland title in 29 years with victory in 2017.

Clare • 2012-Present

Tony Kelly

One of the most naturally gifted players of the modern game, Kelly's creativity and flair have made him the heartbeat of Clare hurling.

Defining Moment

Won Hurler of the Year at just 20 years of age after leading Clare to All-Ireland glory in 2013.
